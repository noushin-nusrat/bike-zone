Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Determining if your helmet is old and needs replacement involves assessing its overall condition. Inspect the helmet for visible signs of damage, such as cracks, dents, or significant wear. If the color has faded noticeably, it may indicate deterioration. Check the straps for fraying or signs of excessive wear, and make sure they securely fasten. If the helmet no longer fits snugly or your head size has changed, it's time for a replacement. Examine the inner foam for any compression or damage. Additionally, stay informed about manufacturer recalls for your specific helmet model. Generally, helmets should be replaced every 5–10 years or sooner if any of these factors are present, ensuring optimal protection during your cycling adventures.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, it's crucial to perform a thorough safety check to ensure everything is in working order. Start by examining the tires for proper inflation and any signs of damage. Check the brakes to ensure they engage smoothly and effectively. Test the gears to make sure they shift smoothly, and inspect the chain for rust or stiffness. Examine the frame for any visible cracks or damage. Additionally, check the tightness of bolts and nuts, including those on the handlebars, seat, and wheels. If the bike has been stored for an extended period, lubricate the chain and other moving parts. Finally, take a short test ride in a safe, controlled environment to assess the overall performance. If any issues persist or if you are unsure about the bike's safety, it's advisable to have it inspected by a professional bike mechanic before regular use.
